Chicken Biryani

Description

Chicken Biryani is a fragrant and richly spiced dish that combines marinated chicken, aromatic basmati rice, and layers of spices, herbs, and fried onions. The preparation begins by marinating chicken pieces in yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, lemon juice, and salt. This mixture is set aside for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to penetrate the meat. Meanwhile, basmati rice is rinsed and partially cooked with whole spices like bay leaf, cloves, cardamom, and cinnamon to infuse it with aroma.

In a deep pot or handi, ghee or oil is heated, and thinly sliced onions are fried until golden brown and crisp — some of these are set aside for garnishing. The marinated chicken is added to the pot and cooked until tender. Then, the half-cooked rice is layered over the chicken, along with saffron-infused milk, mint leaves, coriander, and the reserved fried onions. The pot is sealed and cooked on low heat (dum) for about 20–30 minutes to let the flavors meld together. The result is a heavenly dish with tender chicken, perfectly spiced rice, and a mouthwatering aroma that makes biryani a timeless favorite.

Ingredients

For the Chicken Marinade:

  • Chicken - 500g (bone-in pieces preferred for flavor)
  • Yogurt (curd) - 1 cup
  • Ginger-garlic paste - 1½ tablespoons
  • Red chili powder - 1 teaspoon (adjust to taste)
  • Turmeric powder - ½ teaspoon
  • Garam masala - 1 teaspoon
  • Lemon juice - 1 tablespoon
  • Salt - as required

For the Rice:

  • Basmati rice - 2 cups
  • Bay leaf - 1
  • Cloves - 4-5
  • Green cardamoms - 4
  • Cinnamon stick - 1 inch
  • Salt - to taste
  • Water - as needed (for boiling)

For the Biryani Layering:

  • Onion - 2 large (thinly sliced)
  • Tomato - 1 (chopped, optional)
  • Green chilies - 2 (slit)
  • Mint leaves - a handful
  • Fresh coriander leaves - a handful
  • Ghee or oil - 3 to 4 tablespoons
  • Saffron - a pinch (soaked in 2 tablespoons warm milk)
  • Biryani masala - 1½ teaspoons (optional, for added flavor)

Steps

  1. Marinate the Chicken

    1. In a large bowl, combine:
      • 500g chicken pieces (washed and drained)
      • 1 cup yogurt
      • 1½ tbsp ginger-garlic paste
      • 1 tsp red chili powder
      • ½ tsp turmeric powder
      • 1 tsp garam masala
      • 1 tbsp lemon juice
      • Salt to taste
    2. Mix everything well and let it marinate for at least 30 minutes to 1 hour. (Overnight in the fridge gives best results.)

  2. Parboil the Rice

    1. Wash and soak 2 cups of basmati rice in water for 30 minutes.
    2. Boil water in a large pot with:
      • Salt
      • 1 bay leaf
      • 4-5 cloves
      • 4 cardamoms
      • 1 inch cinnamon stick
    3. Add the soaked rice and cook until it's 70-80% done (it should still have a bite).
    4. Drain the rice and set aside.

  3. Fry the Onions

    1. In a wide, heavy-bottomed pot (biryani pot), heat 3-4 tablespoons of ghee or oil.
    2. Add 2 thinly sliced onions and fry until golden brown and crispy.
    3. Remove half the onions and keep aside for garnishing.

  4. Cook the Chicken

    1. In the same pot (with remaining onions), add the marinated chicken.
    2. Cook on medium flame for about 8-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until chicken is partially cooked and oil separates.

  5. Layer the Biryani

    1. Turn the heat to low.
    2. On top of the chicken, layer the parboiled rice evenly.
    3. Sprinkle on top:
      • Reserved fried onions
      • A handful of chopped mint and coriander leaves
      • Saffron milk (a pinch of saffron soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk)
      • 1-2 tablespoons of ghee
      • Optional: 1½ tsp biryani masala for extra flavor

  6. Dum (Steam Cooking)

    • Method 1 - Traditional (Sealing):

      1. Cover the pot with a tight lid or seal it with dough.
      2. Cook on low heat for 25-30 minutes. You can place a tawa (griddle) under the pot to avoid direct heat.

    • Method 2 - Oven:

      1. Cover the pot with foil and a lid.
      2. Bake in a preheated oven at 180°C (350°F) for 25-30 minutes.

  7. Serve

    1. Let it rest for 10 minutes before opening.
    2. Gently mix layers without breaking the rice.
    3. Serve hot with raita, salad, or boiled eggs.
