Chicken Biryani

Description

Chicken Biryani is a fragrant and richly spiced dish that combines marinated chicken, aromatic basmati rice, and layers of spices, herbs, and fried onions. The preparation begins by marinating chicken pieces in yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, lemon juice, and salt. This mixture is set aside for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to penetrate the meat. Meanwhile, basmati rice is rinsed and partially cooked with whole spices like bay leaf, cloves, cardamom, and cinnamon to infuse it with aroma.

In a deep pot or handi, ghee or oil is heated, and thinly sliced onions are fried until golden brown and crisp — some of these are set aside for garnishing. The marinated chicken is added to the pot and cooked until tender. Then, the half-cooked rice is layered over the chicken, along with saffron-infused milk, mint leaves, coriander, and the reserved fried onions. The pot is sealed and cooked on low heat (dum) for about 20–30 minutes to let the flavors meld together. The result is a heavenly dish with tender chicken, perfectly spiced rice, and a mouthwatering aroma that makes biryani a timeless favorite.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup basmati rice
  • 200g chicken (boneless or with bone)
  • 1 onion (sliced)
  • 1 tomato (chopped)
  • 2 tbsp yogurt
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric
  • 1 tsp biryani masala (or garam masala)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil or ghee
  • Fresh coriander (optional)

Steps

  1. Cook rice in water with a little salt. Drain and keep aside.
  2. In a pan, heat oil, sauté onions until golden.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste, cook for 1 minute.
  4. Add chicken, turmeric, salt, and masala. Cook for 5-7 minutes.
  5. Add tomatoes and yogurt. Cook till chicken is soft.
  6. Layer cooked rice over the chicken. Cover and cook on low for 5 mins.
  7. Garnish with coriander. Serve hot!
